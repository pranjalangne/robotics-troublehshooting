## Electric current

Everything in this world is made up of tiny particles called atoms. These atoms further have smaller parts called electrons which are charged. The flow of these electrons is known as electrid current and is measured in Amperes (A)

For example: Let's say there is a pipe in which water is flowing. Electric current is like the flowing water and pipe is the wire in which it is flowing.

## Voltage

Voltage is the force that causes electrons to flow between two points. Source of voltage can be a battery and is measured in Volts (V).

In our example, if our pipe is connected between a tank and bottle, the water will flow only if the tank has water. The full tank is like our battery

## Closed circuit

For any current to flow, there needs to be a connection or pathway between two points of different voltage. In electronics. this is called a closed circuit,

In our example of flowing water in the pipe, the tank can be like the positive terminal and the empty bottle is like the negative terminal of the battery with the pipe providing a pathway.

## Resistance

Resistance blocks the flow of current in any circuit. It is measured in Ohm (Ω).

If our pipe has some blockage inside it, the water would flow lesser than the original amount. This blockage in the pipe is like resistance.

## In series combination:

• Resistors are connected end to end one after the another  
• The value of electric current in the circuit remains the same  
• Total resistance increases and is the sum of the resistance of all resistors

$$
R _ {t o t a l} = R _ {1} + R _ {2} + \dots + R _ {n}
$$

## In parallel combination

• Resistors are connected with positive and negative terminals of resistr being bundled together  
• The value of voltage in the circuit remains the same  
• The total resistance decreases and is calculated using

$$
R _ {t o t a l} = \frac {1}{R _ {1}} + \frac {1}{R _ {2}} + \dots + \frac {1}{R _ {n}}
$$

Did you know?

Do you ever wonder how solar panels convert sunlight into light energy?

Solar panels use Photo Diodes to convert solar energy into electrical energy.A photo diode consists of a PN region and an intrinsic layer between the p-region and n-region known as the depletion layer. When the light falls on the depletion region it creates an electron-hole pair which stimulates the flow of current.

## Diodes

Diodes are electronic components that are like a valve that prevents the flow of water in the opposite direction. They permit the flow of current in only one direction. Diodes are made up of semi-conductors such as Silicon (Si) or Germanium (Gr and they are often referred to as semi-conductor diodes.

Practical applications of diodes can be seen in a variety of things around us. Some of the most common examples are:

• Solar panels  
• LED lights  
• Cameras  
• Position sensors  
• Barcode scanners  
• Radio devices  
• Remote controllers

## Workings of a Diode

Diodes eithér work on the principle of forward bias or reverse bias. To create a forward bias, the positive terminal of a diode is connected to the positive side of the circuit and the negative terminal is connected to the negative side of the circuit. However, to create a reverse bias, the positive terminal is connected to negative and negative terminal to positive.

## Types of Diodes

There are four common types of diodes;

• P-N junction diode  
• Zener diode.  
• Light Emitting Diodes or LEDs  
• Photo Diodes

Light Emitting Diodes are probably the most common and widely used diodes. They emit light when connected to the circuit. LED also works on forward bias.

Photo diodes are often known as photo detectors and they also operate in reverse bias. The flow of current is based on the light intensity. When the light falls on the PN Junction in the diode it creates an electron-hole pair in the depletion layer which stimulates the flow of current.

## TO TURN MORE THAN ONE LED ON

## IR Sensors

IR sensors (figure 1.24) are light-based sensors that detect any object is in front of the sensor. IR sensors produce infrared light and checks if the light comes back. If it comes back, the robot knows that there is an obstacle in front of the sensor. If the sensor does not detect the light coming back, there is no obstacle in front of the sensor.

The sensor has 3 pins, OUT, GND and Vcc. When the OUT pin has LOW signal, there is an obstacle in front of the sensor and when the OUT pin has HIGH signal, there is no obsta cle in front of the sensor.
